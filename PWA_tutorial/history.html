Origins of PWAs

While Google coined the term Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) in 2015, the core idea traces back to 2007 when Steve Jobs introduced web apps for the iPhone—apps that looked and behaved like native ones, powered by Safari. However, Apple soon pivoted to native app development, launching the App Store later that year.

Google's Reinvention

In 2015, Alex Russel and Frances Berriman reintroduced the concept as PWAs—web experiences combining native app performance with web accessibility. Google and Microsoft heavily promoted PWAs as a bridge between native and web apps.

PWA History

  • 2014: Service Worker specification introduced, enabling offline functionality.
  • 2015: The term “Progressive Web App” was coined by Alex Russell and Frances Berriman.
  • 2016–2018: Major browsers like Chrome, Firefox, and Edge began supporting PWA standards.
  • 2020–present: PWAs gained mainstream adoption; app stores and operating systems began treating them as first-class apps.

Early Success Stories

Major companies quickly embraced the technology:

  • Flipkart (2016): 3× more time spent, 70% higher conversions, 3× lower data use.
  • AliExpress (2016): +104% new user conversions.
  • Twitter Lite (2017): −70% data usage.
  • Uber (2018): Fast web app for developing markets.

Why PWAs Became Important

Before PWAs, users had to choose between the reach of websites and the functionality of native apps. PWAs combine the best of both worlds — the accessibility of the web and the performance of native applications — making them a vital step in the evolution of modern software development.
