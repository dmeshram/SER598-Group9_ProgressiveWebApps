The Evolution of Progressive Web Apps

Progressive Web Apps (PWAs) emerged as a response to the limitations of traditional web and mobile applications. The concept was first introduced by Google engineers in 2015, as browsers started supporting advanced capabilities like offline caching, push notifications, and installable apps.

Timeline

  • 2014: Service Worker specification introduced, enabling offline functionality.
  • 2015: The term “Progressive Web App” was coined by Alex Russell and Frances Berriman.
  • 2016–2018: Major browsers like Chrome, Firefox, and Edge began supporting PWA standards.
  • 2020–present: PWAs gained mainstream adoption; app stores and operating systems began treating them as first-class apps.

Why PWAs Became Important

Before PWAs, users had to choose between the reach of websites and the functionality of native apps. PWAs combine the best of both worlds — the accessibility of the web and the performance of native applications — making them a vital step in the evolution of modern software development.
